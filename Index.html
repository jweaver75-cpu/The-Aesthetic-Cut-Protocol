Macro Composition Overview

The Classic Physique approach utilizes a high-protein, moderate-carb, and low-fat split. This is essential for **muscle sparing** during a caloric deficit while driving fat oxidation.

Breakdown: P (42%) C (37%) F (21%)

Calorie Distribution Across 6 Meals

Calories are strategically front-loaded for sustained energy but **peak around the training window** (Meal 5) to maximize recovery and nutrient uptake. The final meal is slow-digesting.

Key Protein Source Breakdown

Protein intake is diversified to ensure a complete amino acid profile. **Lean Meats** account for the largest proportion, providing dense, high-quality sources.

Nutrient Timing & Purpose Flow

Each of the 6 meals serves a specific function within the 15-hour eating window, ensuring energy availability when needed and slow-release fuel during fasting/sleep.

Non-Negotiable Principles of the Cut

★

Hyper-Hydration

Target 1.5 - 2 Gallons of water daily. This maximizes fat metabolism, reduces water retention, and maintains muscle fullness, which is critical for the "dry" look.

★

Micro-Nutrient Density

Prioritize green, fibrous vegetables like asparagus and broccoli at every main meal. These provide bulk, satiety, and essential vitamins with minimal caloric impact.

★

Strict Meal Adherence

The plan is set for 6 meals to manage hunger and maintain a steady metabolism. **Prep 3 days in advance** to eliminate decision fatigue and ensure consistency.
